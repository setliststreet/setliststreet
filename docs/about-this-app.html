About this App

The Story Behind Setlist Street

This app was created for fun by a Dead and Company fan and Engineering PhD who has attended 25+ shows and was born out of a nerdy fascination with guessing D&C setlists and desire to analyze which songs are played together most often.

How It All Started

This is the first debut. It was hacked together in 3 days before the G60 concerts.

What started as a personal obsession with predicting what songs would be played at Dead & Company shows turned into this full-featured platform for fellow Deadheads to test their knowledge and compete with each other.

The Vision

If you like this app and want to see it for future D&C shows or other bands feel free to like our social media page and leave a comment!

The goal is to create the ultimate platform for music fans to engage with their favorite bands' live performances in a new and exciting way. What started with Dead & Company could expand to other jam bands and artists.

Technical Details

Built by an Engineering PhD who combined technical expertise with deep Dead & Company knowledge to create something unique for the community.

What Powers Setlist Street:

  • Comprehensive setlist database analysis
  • Statistical modeling of song patterns
  • Real-time game mechanics
  • Community-driven features
  • Data-driven insights

The Data Obsession

The creator's fascination with analyzing which songs are played together most often led to building extensive databases of setlist patterns, song frequencies, and transition probabilities.

Some Fun Statistics:

  • 25+ Dead & Company shows attended by the creator
  • Hundreds of setlists analyzed
  • Thousands of song transitions studied
  • 3 days of intensive coding for the G60 debut
  • Countless hours of passionate development

Community First

This app exists because of the incredible Dead & Company community. Every feature is designed with fellow fans in mind, from casual listeners to tour veterans.

Built for Deadheads, by a Deadhead:

  • Understanding of the culture and community
  • Respect for the music and tradition
  • Focus on fun, not just competition
  • Charity integration for giving back
  • Educational aspects about the music

Future Plans

What started as a 3-day hack is just the beginning. The vision includes:

  • Support for other bands and artists
  • Advanced statistical analysis tools
  • Mobile app development
  • Community features and social elements
  • Live show integration
